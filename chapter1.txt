

The map was never meant to be found. It appeared on a storm-soaked morning, tucked beneath the cracked door of a small cottage at the edge of Brindlewick village.
Elian, a young villager known more for curiosity than caution, discovered it while sweeping away the night’s fallen leaves. The parchment was old, its edges darkened
as if singed by fire, and its ink shimmered faintly like dew in moonlight. Strange symbols curled across its surface, leading toward a place no villager dared speak of—the 
Whispering Woods. Legends said the forest changed paths, swallowed voices, and hid truths the world had forgotten. But the map… was calling.

Elara spread the map across her wooden table, smoothing its wrinkled surface with careful hands. 
The strange ink lines seemed to shift slightly as she stared, forming paths that twisted and looped in 
ways no normal forest should. She traced a finger over a marked symbol at the center—an ancient sigil
 she had only heard about in old village tales. It pulsed faintly under her touch, as if responding to
 her presence. The Whispering Woods lay just beyond the eastern hills, but the map suggested a route that
 didn’t match any known path. Something about it felt alive, almost aware of her watching.

As evening settled over the village, Elara made her decision. She packed a small satchel with bread,
a flask of water, and a lantern, her hands steady despite the questions swirling in her mind.
The warnings of the elders echoed faintly in her memory, but curiosity burned stronger than fear. 
With the map folded tightly in her pocket, she stepped beyond the last row of houses and toward the
dark silhouette of the Whispering Woods. The wind shifted as she approached, carrying with it
a whisper—soft, unreadable, and unmistakably calling her forward.

Elara stepped beneath the first arching branches of the Whispering Woods, and the world behind her seemed
to vanish almost instantly, swallowed by dense shadow and unnatural stillness. The air grew cooler,
heavier, carrying a faint hum that was more felt than heard—like the forest itself was breathing around
her. Sunlight struggled to break through the tangled canopy, scattering in broken shards across the
moss-covered ground. Every step she took felt watched, as if unseen eyes moved with her through the 
trees. Despite the tightening knot of fear in her chest, curiosity pushed her forward. The map in her 
pocket felt warmer than it should have been, and somewhere deep in the woods, something answered her
presence with a soft, distant whisper.
